PARKER'S STORY
I KNOW
I DON'T KNOW
Knowing your status gives you control over your actions,
your health, and your future. Regardless of the outcome
of getting tested, knowledge of the results provides an
increased awareness to live your life to the fullest.
There are many reasons why someone might not go get
tested—no access to testing facilities, a lack of a sup-
port group, a fear of a positive result, but not knowing
can come at a cost to quality of life both physically and
psychologically.
I SHARE
I DON'T SHARE
If you know your HIV status, you have a choice of
whether to share it.* Sharing your status, positive or
negative, can help foster an open dialogue around the
disease and encourage other people to know theirs too.
There is still a great deal of stigma surrounding HIV and
45 U.S. states have laws criminalizing HIV exposure so
the choice to share one's status or not can have high
stakes, though total silence from event those you care
about most can be tremendously damaging to one's
mental well-being.
