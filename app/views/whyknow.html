WHY PARKER RIDES
I KNOW
I DON'T KNOW
Knowing your status gives you control over your actions, your health, and your future. Those that effectively manage their HIV are 97% less likely to transmit to others. Regardless of the outcome of getting tested, knowledge of the results provides increased awareness so you can live your life to the fullest.
There are many reasons why someone might not go get tested—no access to testing facilities, lack of a support, and fear of a positive result and telling others are just some of the considerations, but not knowing has costs too.
I SHARE
I DON'T SHARE
If you know your HIV status, you have a choice of whether to share it. Sharing your status, positive or negative, can help foster an open dialogue around the disease and encourage other people to know theirs too.
It's understandable why you may not share; a great deal of stigma still surrounds HIV. Many U.S. States can even prosecute in instances of known HIV exposure. The risks should be balanced with your well being and that of others.
